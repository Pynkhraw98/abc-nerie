Introduction

The North East Regional Institute of Education (NERIE) stands amidst the natural grandeur in Umiam village, about 20 kms from Shillong city. Like other constituent units of the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT), New Delhi, this Regional Institute of Education caters to the educational needs of the eight states of the North East: Assam, Arunachal Pradesh, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Sikkim, and Tripura.

Its history dates back to 1992, when the 'Task force' of the NCERT headed by Madhuri Shah recommended setting up a Regional College for the NCERT in the North East. In 1995, the Field Units of the NCERT at Guwahati and Shillong were merged to form the NERIE at Shillong. Prof. C.L. Anand, the then Vice Chancellor of Arunachal University, was instrumental in preparing the blueprint.

The NERIE functioned as a Field Office for about ten years. In 2005, faculty positions were created and filled, increasing both faculty and administrative staff. With growing capacity, the Institute shifted in May 2012 to its permanent campus in Nongsder village, Umiam.

The building was inaugurated on June 7, 2013, by Dr. M.M. Pallam Raju, then Hon‚Äôble Minister of Human Resource Development, with dignitaries including Prof. R.C. Laloo, Shri Rajarshi Bhattacharya, Prof. Parvin Sinclair, and Prof. A. Sukumar.

Institute Objectives (aligned with NCERT):
  • I. To undertake, aid, promote and coordinate research in all branches of education. NERIE carries out around eight research programmes annually.
  • II. To conduct pre-service training courses. A milestone was achieved in 2015 with the launch of the Two-Year B.Ed course.
  • III. To organize in-service training based on educational needs of the North Eastern states ‚Äî 20 to 25 programmes are conducted per academic session.
  • IV. To develop educational materials and learning resources for students and teachers ‚Äî around 10 such initiatives per year.
  • V. To organize extension services through seminars and conferences at regional and national levels to foster knowledge-sharing.
  • VI. To lead training and research for MHRD flagship programmes like Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan, RMSA, and now the Samagra Shiksha Abhiyan.
