About North Eastern States

Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh, the land of the rising sun in India, is bordered by Bhutan, China, Myanmar, and Assam. Known for its tribal culture and stunning landscapes, it became a full state in 1987. Its forests, rivers, and biodiversity make it a paradise for nature lovers. >>read more

Assam

Located at the heart of northeast India, Assam is rich in tea plantations, wildlife, and cultural heritage. The state capital is Dispur, and it's famous for the mighty Brahmaputra River and Kamakhya Temple. >>read more

Manipur

Known as the "Jewel of India", Manipur is a land of rich culture and scenic beauty. Surrounded by hills and lush valleys, it shares a border with Myanmar and has a diverse population of Meitei, Naga, and Kuki-Chin-Mizo tribes. >>read more

Mizoram

Mizoram, meaning “land of the hill people,” is a beautiful state filled with green hills, rivers, and bamboo forests. It shares borders with Bangladesh and Myanmar. >>read more

Meghalaya

Meghalaya means “abode of clouds” and lives up to its name with high rainfall, cool weather, and waterfalls. It’s home to Cherrapunji and Mawsynram — some of the wettest places on Earth. >>read more

Nagaland

Nagaland is known for its vibrant tribal culture, festivals like Hornbill, and scenic hills. The state is home to several Naga tribes and has a strong tradition of community living. >>read more

Sikkim

Bordering Bhutan, Nepal, and Tibet, Sikkim is a Himalayan state known for its clean environment, Buddhist monasteries, and Mt. Kanchenjunga, India’s highest peak. >>read more

Tripura

Tripura, a culturally rich state, is known for palaces, temples, and tribal heritage. It shares an extensive border with Bangladesh and is home to many indigenous communities. >>read more
